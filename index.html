🎯 Why I’m Running for URSA President

I’m running for URSA President because I’ve experienced, and seen around me, how many students are quietly struggling, whether it’s managing finances, dealing with academic issues, or just trying to find the right support on campus.

When I talk to students, I often hear the same thing: “We don’t know where to go,” or “Support exists, but it’s hard to access.” That really stayed with me. It made me realize that we don’t just need more support, we need better connection, better communication, and real action.

I decided to run because I genuinely believe we can do better as a student community. URSA should be more visible, more transparent, and more focused on what students actually need in their daily lives.

For me, this isn’t just about a position, it’s about making sure every student feels supported, heard, and part of a stronger, more connected campus.

📊 Transparency & Accountability

I am committed to bringing full transparency to URSA’s financial decisions, including costs, expenditures, scholarships, grants, and all existing funds. Students deserve to clearly understand how their fees are being used and how resources are allocated.

I will work to publish regular, easy-to-understand financial reports, ensure open communication, and create opportunities for student input in funding decisions. My goal is to build trust by making URSA’s finances more visible, accountable, and aligned with the real needs of students.
